Background

Data

  • Our data was provided by: https://vincentarelbundock.github.io/Rdatasets/doc/mosaicData/Weather.html
  • The data is an average of different weather patterns collected every day from 2016-2017 in 5 major cities: Auckland, New Zealand | Beijing, China | Chicago, USA | Mumbai, India | San Diego, USA
  • Weather patterns include: Temperature, Dewpoint, Humidity, Wind, and Weathering Events

Shiny App

weather_mean <- cbind(weather_temp, weather_dewpt, weather_humidty, weather_wind) #combining yearly average into one table
weather_cleaner <- weather_mean %>% 
  select(-c(4,6,8)) %>% 
  pivot_longer(Average_Temp:Average_Wind,
               names_to = "Measure",
               values_to = "Value")
weather_cleaner
  • The Shiny App allows for a visual representation of the yearly comparisons between each city for temperature, dewpoint, humidity, and wind.
  • We had to make multiple tables of each category and combine them. Then, tidy the data using pivot longer.
  • The Shiny App is present in its own file in our Repository.

Average Monthly Temperatures for All Cities for 2016

  • To break things down further, here is a look at the average temperature for each city in 2016.
  • It is evident that Auckland lies in the Southern Hemisphere while the other cities lie in the Northern Hemisphere.
  • Auckland increases temperature while the other cities average temperatures decrease and vice versa.

Average Monthly Temperatures for All Cities for 2017

  • Similar temperature patterns are shown in 2017 with slight fluctuations.
  • We also notice that Mumbai has overall the highest temperature because it is closest to the equator.

Average Monthly Dewpoints for All Cities for 2016

  • Average dewpoint percentages show similar results to average temperatures as dewpoint is defined as the measure of how much moisture is in the air.

Average Monthly Dewpoints for All Cities for 2017

  • Again here is the overall year of 2017 showing the dewpoint monthly average percentages for each of the 5 cities.

Average Monthly Humidity for All Cities for 2016

  • Humidity varies from the two previous graphs. As seen, Auckland has a consistently high humidity percentage while the other cities have greater fluctuation.
  • This is also in fact due to the fact that Auckland lies closest to the equator and does not vary in weather patterns as much.

Average Monthly Humidity for All Cities for 2017

  • In 2017, the overall humidity greatly resembles that of 2016 for these major cities.
